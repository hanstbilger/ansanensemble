ANSAN ENSEMBLE is a musical family connected through sound, language, culture, and deep friendship. The group is led by Siamak Mir Sheibani Moghaddam, an Iranian singer and instrumentalist who weaves Persian classical poems by Rumi, Hafez, and others into middle eastern-inflected songs that keen, meditate, and erupt with joy. Moghaddam is joined by a globe-spanning group of improvisers, each expert in a different musical tradition. Israeli oudist Doron Furman is well versed in the melodic lyricism of Arabic maqams. Percussionist Matan Goldstien, also from Israel, brings a conservatory-trained ear for European classical percussion and a rich understanding of his homeland’s oblong grooves. Dr. Vijay Kannan studied Carnatic singing in his native India before picking up the bansuri flutes that float and dart through Ansan’s compositions. And contrabassist Hans Bilger was raised in the jazz and folk traditions of America. In Persian, Ansan means “the other way.” True to its name, the artists of this group meet at a bustling musical crossroads—and then take a different path altogether.
